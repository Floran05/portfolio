About

Hi ! My name is Floran, and I'm a video game developer, currently studying for a master's degree at Gaming Campus. My academic background is rooter in computer science, and I already hold a first master's degree in this field.   • Drone simulator

    As you'll read below in the sesion describing my hobbies, I'm a FPV drone pilot. This discipline has on drawback: a small piloting error can lead to a drone crash, and potentially high repair costs. One way of avoiding such errors is to train on a simulator, but simulators also often have a drawback, which is the fidelity of physics to reality, and more specifically the effect of gravity. In most simulators, when you let the drone fall (it's common to have to put the throttle to minimum), it loses altitude much more slowly than in real-life conditions.

    This problem made me want to test for myself and create a prototype drone flight model on Unreal Engine, to understant how the physics engine works and, by extension, why most simulators suffer from this inaccuracy compared to reality.

    The answer (at least in the case of Unreal Engine), is a coefficient used to simulate air friction during the fall. This method provides an approximation, but the result is still a long way from reality, at least in the context of drone piloting, which requires enormous precision.

  • Paragon

    Just before Epic Games released Fortnite, one of their teams was working on a third-person MOBA called Paragon. Available in early access, I started playing (it's too hard to start League of Legends this late, there are too many champions to learn !) and quickly got hoocked: it has great art direction, incredible character design and thirs-person gameplay that's far more immersive thant the isometric view of Riot Games' MOBA.

    However, shortly after Fortnite was released, Epic Games annonced that development of Paragon would not be continuing. Aware of the work they were abandoning, the studio made many of the assets used in the game freely available on the Unreal Engine marketplace.

    Saddened by this news, I decided to redevelop the game so that I could continue playing with friends. This project enabled me to discover Unreal Engine and learn how game creation on this engine works. After three years of inching forward, I finally gave up on this project, which wasn't really mine after all, but which nevertheless enabled me to learn an enormous amount.

Paragon: details of some implemented features

Deck builder

Whereas League of Legends uses a system of items store, which players can purchase as the game proggresses, Paragon is based on a card deck system. In order to adapt to different team compositions, players must first build several decks. During this process, you need to equip gems (left side of the interface) and cards (central part of the interface). Gems and cards have characteristics that influence the character's stats, or offer passives. Cards can also provide an active skill. They all have an affinity that defines their play style (e.g. Chaos (Wold) ⇒ damage, Growth (Flower) ⇒ defense), and a deck can only contain cards of two different affinities.

Demonstration

In-game shop

Once players have earned 1000 coins during the game, they can spend them on an attribute point (Agility, Vitality or Intellect). When the player has enough attribute points (the cost is indicated in the top right corner of each card), he can equip a card in one of the three solts.

Demonstration

Levels and abilities

Characters can gain experience and level up by performing various actions (killing an enemy player, destroying a turret, etc.). Each level up allows the player to unlock or improve a character's skill.

Demonstration

Turrets and minions

To win the game, players must destroy the opposing team's Core (a building in the player's spawn area), which is protected by turrets on each line of the map. Players must therefore advance along each line, destroying the turrets blocking the way without beingg killed by them. They must allow the minions to advance, so that they can take the turrets' damage instead of the player

Demonstration

Vision system

Players can move discreetly around the map to surprise their opponents. To do this, they need to stay out of direct line-of-sight with an opposing player. If an opponent is spotted, the whole team is informed of his position.

Demonstration

Quick communication system

A quick communication system allows players to transmit essential information to the whole team without having to write a message in the chat. The system also allows you to send a message that will be automatically translated into the language of the other players.
